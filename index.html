The Funnel Problem

They disappear at the moment of purchase

Blomsterlandet shows up when people ask "what are the best garden stores?" (discovery). They even win the head-to-head comparison against Plantagen. But when someone asks "who has the best guarantee and delivery for perennials?" — the actual purchase decision — Blomsterlandet is mentioned by zero out of three AI models. Scandiaplants, Plantagen, and niche stores take over.

Key Findings

Strong in Swedish discovery

When users search in Swedish, Blomsterlandet appears across all four AI engines. ChatGPT even ranks them #1 for "bästa trädgårdsbutiken online Sverige". The brand is well-established in Swedish-language AI responses.

Wins the head-to-head comparison

When users ask "Blomsterlandet or Plantagen?", ChatGPT recommends Blomsterlandet as better value. Gemini gives a balanced comparison but highlights Blomsterlandet's Swedish-grown focus. This is strong — the brand holds up under direct scrutiny.

Invisible for feature-specific purchase queries

When queries shift to specific buying criteria — plant guarantees, delivery options, perennial selection — Blomsterlandet vanishes. Their website lacks structured, citation-friendly content about these features. Scandiaplants wins on "guarantee" (36-month). Plantagen wins on "delivery". Blomsterlandet's 1-year plant guarantee exists but isn't surfaced by any AI model.

Invisible in English on ChatGPT (with web search)

When ChatGPT uses web search for English queries, Blomsterlandet disappears entirely. Niche players like Ormbunker, Gardens4You, and Scandiaplants dominate instead.

Perplexity is the weakest platform

Perplexity only mentions Blomsterlandet for Swedish queries. For English and purchase queries, Plantagen, IKEA, and Plantazon dominate. Perplexity relies heavily on structured web content and citation-friendly pages.

Why This Matters

The last mile problem

People don't buy from one AI query. The typical journey is:

"What are the best garden stores?" → "Blomsterlandet or Plantagen?" → "Who has the best plant guarantee?" → purchase

Blomsterlandet wins stages 1 and 2 — but loses the customer at stage 3. The person who was leaning toward Blomsterlandet now sees Scandiaplants with a 36-month guarantee and switches. All the brand awareness work is wasted if you're invisible at the purchase moment.

Fix: Create dedicated, structured pages for plant guarantees, delivery policies, and product-specific landing pages (perennials, fruit trees, etc.) with clear, quotable claims that AI models can retrieve and cite.

Action Plan

What Blomsterlandet should do to improve AI search visibility.

Immediate (0-4 weeks)

  1. Create English-language landing pagesHigh
    ChatGPT and Perplexity can't find Blomsterlandet for English queries. A well-structured English "About Us" and "Shop Plants Online in Sweden" page would provide content for AI models to cite. Focus on phrases like "garden store Sweden" and "buy plants online Sweden".
  2. Add structured data (Organization + LocalBusiness schema)High
    AI models with web search extract structured data to determine which brands to recommend. Add JSON-LD schema with brand description, service area (Sweden), product categories, and store locations.
  3. Publish a "Why Blomsterlandet" comparison pageHigh
    AI models often source from comparison and "best of" content. Create a page that positions Blomsterlandet against alternatives (Plantagen, online-only stores) with clear differentiators: store count, expert advice, plant guarantee, sustainability.

Short-term (1-3 months)

  1. Build citation-friendly content hubMedium
    AI models cite pages with clear, quotable paragraphs. Create guides like "Complete Guide to Gardening in Sweden", "Best Plants for Swedish Climate Zones". Structure with H2s, short paragraphs, and data points that AI can extract.
  2. Get listed in English-language "best of Sweden" roundupsMedium
    ChatGPT with web search pulls from blog posts and roundups. Pitch travel/lifestyle bloggers who write about Swedish shopping. Even one well-ranking English article mentioning Blomsterlandet would improve retrieval.
  3. Optimize for Perplexity specificallyMedium
    Perplexity favors structured, citation-heavy pages. Ensure product category pages have clear meta descriptions, intro paragraphs, and internal linking. Consider adding an FAQ section with conversational Q&A that matches how people query AI.

Ongoing

  1. Monitor AI visibility monthlyOngoing
    Run this same GEO audit monthly to track progress. AI model responses change as they update their training data and web search indexes. Track position changes and new competitor entries.
  2. Create llms.txt fileOngoing
    Add a /llms.txt file (similar to robots.txt) that provides a structured description of the brand for AI crawlers. Early adoption signals — some models are beginning to use this.

Scoring

Each query-model combination is one "check". A brand receives a "mentioned" score if it appears anywhere in the AI's response text. Position tracking (#1, #2, etc.) reflects the order in which brands are listed. The overall visibility score is: (mentioned checks / total checks) × 100.

Total API Cost

12 API calls across 4 models: $0.44 in DataForSEO charges. Full audit including Claude Code compute: approximately $3-5 total.

Limitations

AI responses are non-deterministic. Running the same query again may produce different results. This audit is a snapshot, not a guarantee. Temperature was set to 0.3 (low randomness) for consistency. Web search results depend on each model's real-time index.
