The Big Picture

/tips-rad/ is the GEO weapon

Blomsterlandet's editorial content at /tips-rad/tradgard/perenner/ is directly cited by Claude and Gemini as an authoritative source. Their article "Fina perenner för skuggiga lägen" was cited 6 times in a single Claude response. This is not just a mention — AI models are quoting their content to users.

Wins the purchase moment

When someone asks "var kan jag köpa perenner online med hemleverans?" — Blomsterlandet appears in all 4 AI models. Claude and Gemini list them #1. Perplexity even surfaces their current 20% discount on perenner. Unlike the v1 audit, they don't disappear at the purchase stage when using real queries.

ChatGPT is a black hole for Swedish queries

ChatGPT (GPT-4o) does not trigger web search for most Swedish product questions. It answers from training data only — listing generic plant names without citing any brand or store. This means ChatGPT users get no store recommendations at all for research queries. Only the direct purchase query "var kan jag köpa" triggered a store list (from memory, no web search).

Perplexity is the citation king

Perplexity cites Blomsterlandet content for 3 of 4 queries — including product guides AND the store/purchase page. It pulls the richest data (current discounts, delivery times, product pages). But for "perenner i skugga", Hortogreen and Wexthuset win the citation instead.

The Real User Journey in AI Search

How people actually use AI for garden shopping:

1. "Vilka perenner passar i skugga?" — Claude answers using Blomsterlandet's guide. User reads plant recommendations with Blomsterlandet as the cited expert.

2. "Var kan jag köpa dessa perenner online?" — All models list Blomsterlandet. Claude links directly to blomsterlandet.se/produkter/vaxter/utomhus/perenner/

3. The user clicks through or remembers the brand.

This is the ideal GEO funnel: be the source of expert content in stage 1, then be the recommended store in stage 2. Blomsterlandet achieves this on Claude and Gemini — but not on ChatGPT.

What makes these pages citable?

These pages share common traits that make AI models prefer them as sources:

  • Clear, specific H1s that match search queries exactly ("Fina perenner för skuggiga lägen")
  • Short, quotable paragraphs — Claude directly quotes sentences from these pages
  • Structured plant recommendations with species names, care tips, and placement advice
  • Swedish-language authority on a .se domain with established topical relevance

Pages NOT Cited (Opportunity)

Guarantee & delivery pages

Blomsterlandet's 1-year plant guarantee and delivery policies are mentioned by AI but never with a direct citation link. Creating a dedicated /tips-rad/vaxtgaranti/ or /leverans/ page with structured, quotable content would give AI models something to cite when users ask about guarantees.

English-language content

No English pages exist. When queries are in English, Blomsterlandet disappears from ChatGPT and Perplexity. Even a single well-structured English landing page would be retrievable.

What ChatGPT Actually Does

"Vilka perenner passar i skugga?" No web search · No brands

ChatGPT answers from training data only. Lists 7 plant species (Astilbe, Hosta, Löjtnantshjärta, etc.) with correct botanical info — but no store references, no links, no product pages. Users get helpful gardening advice but zero purchase pathway.

"Vilka perenner blommar hela sommaren?" No web search · No brands

Same pattern. Lists 9 species correctly (Echinacea, Salvia, Rudbeckia, etc.). Zero brand mentions. If a user follows up with "var kan jag köpa?", ChatGPT will then list stores from memory — but the initial research query has no brand association.

"Var kan jag köpa perenner online?" No web search · But lists stores from memory

ChatGPT lists 5 stores from training data: Plantagen (#1), Odla.nu (#2), Wexthuset (#3), Blomsterlandet (#4), Zetas (#5). No links, no current offers, no citations. This is brand recall from pre-training, not live data.

Why This Matters

ChatGPT is the most-used AI assistant globally. When Swedish users ask it about gardening, they get accurate plant advice but no connection to stores. The research phase (the majority of queries) is a brand-free zone on ChatGPT.

This isn't something Blomsterlandet can directly fix — it's a ChatGPT behavior difference for non-English queries. But it means: the other 3 models (Claude, Gemini, Perplexity) carry 100% of the GEO value for Swedish research queries. Optimizing for those platforms matters more than ChatGPT for this market.

Action Plan

Priorities based on what the data actually shows — not generic GEO advice.

Double Down on What Works

  1. Expand /tips-rad/ content for high-volume queriesHigh
    The existing articles are already being cited as sources. Create more for the top queries: "tåliga perenner" (480/mo), "perenner soligt läge" (590/mo), "blå perenner" (480/mo), "barrotade perenner" (590/mo). Each well-structured guide = a new citable source for AI models.
  2. Structure existing guides for better AI extractionHigh
    Claude already quotes Blomsterlandet's content verbatim. Optimize for this: clear H2 headings matching query patterns, short paragraphs with species names and care tips in the first 2 sentences, data points (bloom period, height, hardiness zone) that AI can extract. FAQ sections with conversational Q&A.
  3. Keep fråga-experten.blomsterlandet.se activeMedium
    Perplexity cited the fråga-experten Q&A page. This user-generated content is valuable for long-tail AI queries. Ensure it remains crawlable and well-structured during any replatforming.

Close the Gaps

  1. Win the "perenner i skugga" query on PerplexityMedium
    Perplexity cites Hortogreen and Wexthuset for this query instead of Blomsterlandet. The existing Blomsterlandet article exists but isn't being retrieved. Possible fixes: stronger meta description, add structured data, increase internal linking to this page, add pricing/product links within the guide.
  2. Create a dedicated guarantee/delivery pageMedium
    AI models mention Blomsterlandet's 1-year guarantee and hemleverans but can't cite a source page. A well-structured /om-oss/vaxtgaranti/ page would give AI something quotable for purchase-decision queries.
  3. Monitor ChatGPT web search behaviorLow
    ChatGPT's lack of web search for Swedish queries is a platform limitation. Re-test monthly — if ChatGPT starts searching for Swedish queries, Blomsterlandet's existing content should rank well (it already works on Claude/Gemini/Perplexity).
